Old Barn Poem. The old barn now stands deserted down next to the water well alone in a field of grass and weed by the old sign that says for sale. He wanted to buy the old barn that sits out by the highway.

Deep in its hay the leghorn hides a round white nest. Age really is meaningless just year after year. Grey is just a color in one s eyes.

I told him right off he was crazy.

Tons upon tons the browngreen fragrant hay oerbrims the mows beyond the timewarped eaves up to the rafters where the spider weaves. The weathered old barn a stranger came by the other day with an offer that set me to thinking. More than just buildings they are testaments to lives and a way of life like shells cast up on the beach. The wisdom of a child.
